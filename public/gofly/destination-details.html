Samarkand, one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in Central Asia, is a jewel of the ancient Silk Road. Known for its stunning Islamic architecture, vibrant bazaars, and rich cultural heritage, Samarkand offers visitors a glimpse into the grandeur of the Timurid Empire. The city's iconic Registan Square, with its three magnificent madrasahs, stands as a testament to the architectural genius of the 15th century. From the breathtaking Gur-e-Amir Mausoleum to the astronomical wonders of Ulugh Beg Observatory, Samarkand is a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts and cultural explorers.
